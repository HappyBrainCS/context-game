## Context

Money is the most fundamental coordination tool in modern society, yet most people don't understand how it's created, who controls it, or why its value changes over time. This question asks what's structurally wrong with the current global monetary system — not surface-level complaints about prices being high, but the underlying incentives, power structures, and design flaws baked into how money works today.

## Suggested Angles

- **Monetary sovereignty and central banking:** Who should have the power to create money, and what checks should exist on that power?
- **Inflation as a hidden tax:** Is inflation a bug or a feature of the current system? Who benefits and who loses?
- **The Cantillon Effect:** How does new money entering the economy at specific points create unfair advantages for early recipients?
- **Debt-based money:** Should money be created as debt (loans), and what does this imply about required economic growth?
- **Global reserve currency dynamics:** What problems arise from one nation's currency being the world's reserve?
- **Alternatives and fixes:** Can Bitcoin, gold, or other systems solve these problems, or do they introduce new ones?
