## Summary

The problem with money isn't just that central bankers print it out of thin air — it's that our entire monetary system is structurally incompatible with a technological world. Technology is relentlessly deflationary: every year, we can produce more with less. But our fiat system needs inflation to survive, so it fights technology every step of the way. The result is a world where innovation makes the rich richer (via asset inflation) while ordinary people feel squeezed. Bitcoin breaks this cycle: fixed supply, no printing, honest money that lets technology's benefits flow to everyone. [Source: Jeff Booth, The Price of Tomorrow]

## Details

### Technology Is Deflationary by Nature
Jeff Booth's core insight in *The Price of Tomorrow* is that we live in an era of exponential technological progress. Computing power, energy efficiency, AI, manufacturing — everything gets cheaper and better over time. In a natural economy, prices would fall as technology improves. That's abundance. Technology wants to make things cheaper; our monetary system fights this at every turn. [Source: Jeff Booth, The Price of Tomorrow]

### Our Money System Fights Progress
Central banks target 2% inflation. They create money to fight deflation — because deflation would expose the flaw in a system built on ever-expanding debt. So they print, and print, and print. This doesn't stop technology from improving — it just captures the gains. The cheapening that should benefit everyone instead inflates asset prices (housing, stocks) that primarily benefit those who already have capital. The invisible tax has a specific mechanism: each new dollar created enters the economy at the top — through banks and financial institutions — before prices adjust. By the time it reaches ordinary wages and savings, prices have already risen. No vote. No legislation. Just monetary engineering.

### Bitcoin Is the Exit
21 million. No more. No less. For the first time, we have money that cannot be printed, diluted, or manipulated. It aligns perfectly with a deflationary technological world: as technology improves and productivity increases, each bitcoin becomes more valuable — not because of speculation, but because there are more goods chasing the same money supply. That's honest money.

### Booth's Two-Case Hedge
Bitcoin hedges both futures. If governments keep printing and getting away with it, bitcoin is hard-money inflation insurance. If the system eventually cracks under the debt load, bitcoin is already there as a global, decentralized, functional monetary network that doesn't depend on any bank or state. [Source: Jeff Booth on Citizen Bitcoin, casebitcoin.com]
