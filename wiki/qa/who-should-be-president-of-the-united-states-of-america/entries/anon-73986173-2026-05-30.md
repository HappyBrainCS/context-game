## Summary

The United States should not have a president. The entire premise — that an individual or small group of elected officials can know what's best for an entire country — has always been a flawed design. We need a new system where people can collaborate directly without unqualified middlemen deciding how the entire world operates.

## Details

The presidency concentrates enormous power in one person, creates perverse incentives (campaign finance, political maneuvering over good governance), and assumes expertise that no single human can possess across all the domains a nation touches — economics, foreign policy, healthcare, education, defense, technology, environment.

The deeper problem is structural: representative democracy asks citizens to choose people, not ideas. The chosen individuals then operate with limited accountability until the next election, insulated from real-time feedback and correction.

A better system would be:
- **Direct participation** — people contribute to decisions that affect them, mediated by tools that make collaboration feasible at scale
- **Merit over personality** — ideas compete on merit rather than which candidate has the best charisma or fundraising
- **Distributed expertise** — decisions come from people closest to the problem, not a centralized command structure
- **Continuous accountability** — feedback loops that operate in real time, not once every 4-6 years

## Open Questions

- What specific mechanisms could replace the presidency? Direct democracy? AI-mediated consensus? Something else?
- Is this idea scalable to a country of 330+ million people, or does it require a different organizational scale?
- What would the transition look like? Can a system be changed from within, or does it require building something new alongside the old?
